What is this "First Aid" envelope in the cover of my text?

First of all, it is not important. Inside the envelope of a new textbook there is a card with a registration number to give you password protected access to web applets coordinated with the textbook. The registration number may only be used once to set up your account. Used textbooks might not have the card.
Read more . . .

If you have a used textbook the registration number might already have been used, in which case the previous owner was supposed to write the login and password on the card for your use. If that information is present, maybe the account will still work. If you have no login and password, or an invalid login and password combination, and the registration code has been used, then when you try to register you will get an "Invalid regcode" error. Since this text is now out of print, probably most codes have been used once and are now invalid. If you want to try your card, the URL for signing up for an account has changed from that printed on the card to:

http://bcs.wiley.com/he-bcs/Books?action=index&itemId=0471447951&bcsId=1559

After clicking on the above link, click on "Login/Register" and follow instructions on screen and/or on your card from there.

But do not worry if your registration number does not work. Do not pay to get a new registration code The publisher now offers the applets for free.
