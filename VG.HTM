A CHRISTIAN UNIVERSITY? WHAT'S THAT?
The course you have been invited to visit will be taught at Dordt University, a small private university, "in the cornfields of Iowa." The most distinctive aspect of this university is it's dedication to Christianity. This offer to visit is not an explicit attempt at evangelism. The course will be on-target for the engineering topic you expect. You do not need to be a Christian to visit. All we ask is that you be respectful of our freedom to believe and we will do our utmost to return our respect for you, whatever you believe (or don't believe). Professor De Boer's courses, being engineering courses, do not tread heavily into theology (or at least so he thinks), but be prepared for some times of prayer in class and some discussion as we relate our course to our faith.

If you really want to get into the nitty gritty of Dordt University, read their "Task" and "Framework" documents. Those are probably TMI for this context, but anyway, there you could go.

YOUR FIRST DAY AT CLASS
You might like to dive right into the first live lecture of the semester. In that case, the link that is about 3/4 of the way down the public portal page titled, "Zoom in to the live classroom here" is for you. This link will be functional from about 10 minutes before the announced start time for class to the end of class. Example: Suppose the class starts on Friday at 8:00 AM Central time (USA). You live in California. The class will start at 6:00 AM Pacific time (USA) (sorry!) The link will be functional from about 5:50 AM Pacific time to the end of the class each day when class is held.

But maybe you don't want to watch live because the hours are inconvenient for you. Then the link titled, "Slides and videos for each class period" is for you. After class Professor De Boer will upload the Zoom recordings to YouTube for distribution. He will usually do this in the late afternoon of his day on the day of the class or earlier if he gets a chance. From then on the video of the class will be available and the slides he used in the lecture can also be separately downloaded as a PDF file.

Feel free to explore all the other links to the extent of your interest. If you would like to do homework, you will need access to a textbook to get the problem statements. Professor De Boer cannot grade your homework, but he will post solutions after the students turn in their work. Depending on his workload, Professor De Boer might be willing to offer help on some of the homework.

DECORUM
If you join the live classroom your microphone and camera will normally be turned off when you join. Professor De Boer chooses this setting so that nobody is accidentally surprised by an open camera or microphone. Professor De Boer welcomes you to turn on your camera and, if you want to join in classroom discussion, the microphone.

If you turn on the camera please be dressed in normal work or school clothing and be aware of what might show in the video frame besides yourself, including other people that might show in the background, posters on the walls, etc. If any of this is distracting, please keep your camera turned off. For example, an occasional person walking past is OK. A pet constantly begging for attention is probably not OK. An NSFW poster on the wall is not OK, not even if it far away from the camera. Similarly, if you enter the classroom discussion, your language should be free of vulgarities, profanities, and blasphemies.

TECHNICAL STUFF
We will be using the Zoom platform for the live classroom. I highly recommend that you use a laptop or desktop computer if you join the Zoom livestream because I have formatted my slides and lectures for a large screen. (But a smartphone ought to connect and do something useful.)

The Zoom "desktop client" (also works on a laptop) has a convenient feature called "push to talk." If your microphone is turned off (muted) and you press the spacebar then your microphone will unmute for as long as you hold the spacebar down. This is a very nice way to enter the discussion without contributing background noise into the entire stream when you are not talking. You may need to enable push-to-talk if you want to use it.

SAFETY and SECURITY
There are no guarantees in this world. Visitors should do their part to protect their access to the course they are visiting. Please keep your access URL to yourself. If others want to join the course, have them email professor De Boer and make their request directly. Yes, the course's access URL is totally public and can be found with a well-formed Google search. However the servers Professor De Boer is using will not be able to handle the load if the URLs go viral.

Professor De Boer will not be using university resources in service of visitors. This means that to keep access for visitors free he will be using public servers like YouTube, InfinityFree, and Github that offer free hosting services. It is not possible to maintain high security with these servers at this price-point ($0). Denial-of-service attacks and such, if they happen, could unfortunately result in the loss of visitor access to his courses. (Students enrolled for credit will have access to the university servers and course management system, all of which is secure and behind a paywall.)

Trust is the foundation of truth and fulfillment in life. Please do your part.
