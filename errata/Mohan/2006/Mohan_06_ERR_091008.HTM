Page 2-32. Problem 2-17
"Load" and "source" and VR and VS are interchanged in a few places.
A corrected version of the problem statement is:

In a radial system represented by the circuit of Fig. 2-17a,
X = 1.5 . Consider the source voltage to be constant at VS = 100∠0º. Calculate and plot |VR|/|VS| if the load varies in a range from 0 to 1 kW a the following three power factors: unity, 0.9 (lagging), 0.9 (leading).
This problem is supposed to show that loads with low or even slightly leading power factors can be used to help maintain or even raise the voltage at the load. Conversely, even slightly lagging power factors are hard on the system and the voltage sags.

(posted 10/06/2009. Updated 10/08/2009 This errata has been corrected in some printings of the text.)
